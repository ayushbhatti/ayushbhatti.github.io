01
🔬 Open-World AI · Flagship Research

Open Horizons: Evaluating Deep Models in the Wild

A rigorous empirical study of how modern deep visual representations behave under open-world conditions — where models must classify known classes, reject unknowns, and adapt to new categories over time. Evaluates ResNet-50, ConvNeXt, and CLIP ViT-B/16 across MSP, Energy, Mahalanobis, and kNN scoring functions. Benchmarks FSCIL strategies (SPPR, OrCo, ConCM) under Few-Shot Class-Incremental protocols. Key finding: CLIP embeddings dominate open-set separability, and structured prototype methods (OrCo, ConCM) best mitigate catastrophic forgetting.

PyTorch CLIP ResNet-50 AUROC FSCIL OSR

🧠 Transfer Learning

Image Classification with Transfer Learning

Applied VGG16 (ImageNet-pretrained) to MNIST digit classification, adapting grayscale data to a full RGB pipeline with resizing, normalization, and data augmentation. Achieved ~92% validation accuracy through frozen feature extraction and custom fully-connected heads. Demonstrates end-to-end fine-tuning methodology.

An ML-driven algorithmic trading system using SMA crossover signals (30-day vs 100-day) with logistic regression to predict buy/sell decisions for AAPL. Built with Yahoo Finance data, backtested on 2022 returns, and visualized cumulative performance. Demonstrates applied ML beyond traditional CV domains.

Neural network-based reconstruction of defective printing block images from a historical writing era. Uses deep learning with Python to reconstruct damaged regions and visualize pixel-level differences between defective and trained-perfect images. Ongoing research at University of Arizona.
